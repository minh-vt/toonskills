## I. Style Core Definition (Generative Underlying Logic)
All art prompts, specifications, and generated content for this style are strictly limited to:
-  **Sinicized Cyberpunk 3D Rendering**: Centered on 3D modeling and rendering technology, it deeply integrates traditional Eastern aesthetics with the Cyberpunk sci-fi core. It localizes the fusion of Chinese mortise-and-tenon structures, ink-wash aesthetics, traditional patterns, ancient forms with holographic lighting, mechanical structures, neon effects, and futuristic technology. It rejects superficial element stacking.
-  **3D Ultra-Fine Texture**: 8K high-precision models, 4K ultra-clear PBR physical material rendering, next-gen game-level topology. Textures such as metal, fabric, jade, and holographic materials are rendered with extreme detail and clarity.
-  **Sinicized Cyberpunk Exclusive Color Palette**: Based on cyan-green, vermilion, indigo, gilded gold, and ink black, paired with low-saturation Chinese neon tones (cinnabar neon, stone-cyan holographic, gilded glow). It avoids fluorescent color bleeding, balancing elegance with a futuristic feel.
-  **Eastern Cyberpunk Aesthetic Atmosphere**: A blend of ink-wash imagery (the interplay of void and reality) with Cyberpunk lighting. Features negative space composition, Chinese symmetry, volumetric lighting with misty rain, and holographic projections, perfectly fusing poetic space with futuristic cityscapes.
-  **Sinicized Cyberpunk Character Modeling**: 3D next-gen ancient-style modeling, Eastern classical facial bone structures, and traditional Hanfu designs modified with Cyberpunk functional elements. Makeup is elegant, incorporating cyber-glow effects, while hairstyles are elaborate and paired with mechanical accessories, maintaining authentic Eastern charm.
-  **Cinematic Top-Tier Lighting**: UE5/Blender Cycles high-end rendering, including volumetric lighting, ambient occlusion (AO), global illumination, ray tracing, depth of field, motion blur, and LUT color calibration for a cinematic lens language.

### Scope of Application
-  AI art generation for Sinicized Cyberpunk 3D themes (character character sheets, scene concepts, prop design, storyboards, game CG, film posters, etc.)
-  Visual creation requiring next-gen 3D quality, Eastern aesthetic kernels, and Cyberpunk sci-fi atmosphere.
-  Commercial applications such as game art, film concept design, animation shorts, and digital collectibles with a Sinicized Cyberpunk theme.

### Prohibited Content
-  Realistic photography/photo-realism (unless specified otherwise)
-  Pure Western Cyberpunk/Western fantasy/Gothic/Victorian styles, or disjointed pasting without an Eastern kernel.
-  Modern urban daily life elements or pure sci-fi machinery lacking Chinese cultural elements.
-  Low-precision models, rough topology, blurry textures, mesh clipping, or deformed structures.
-  Overly saturated fluorescent colors, high color bleeding, strong digital noise, or low-contrast murky visuals.
-  Westernized facial structures that contradict Eastern classical bone aesthetics, deformed human bodies, or unnatural limb proportions.
-  Vulgarity, nudity, Hanfu styles modified incorrectly, or stacking of elements without cultural substance.

## I. Overlay Principles (Core Rules for Both Scenarios)

1. **Unchanged Facial Features** — The facial features must remain identical to the base model after the overlay. Any facial shift, distortion, or stylized tampering is strictly prohibited.
2. **Unchanged Pose** — Maintain the base model's natural standing pose. Any changes to pose, movement, or body posture are strictly prohibited.
3. **Layer-by-Layer Control** — Each layer is described independently to facilitate layer-by-layer replacement.
4. **Unified Style** — All apparel and styling elements must adhere to the same style fusion logic.
5. **No Loss in Quality** — The rendering and texture quality after styling must not be lower than the base model.
6. **Strictly Apparel & Styling Scope** — Only overlay makeup, hairstyles, clothing, and accessories; introducing props, scenes, environments, or actions is prohibited.
7. **One-Click Dual-Scenario Adaptation** — Defaults to pure traditional Chinese style when no explicit cyberpunk/urban cues are present. Automatically switches to the Chinese cyberpunk urban system when relevant cues are detected, without requiring reconstruction of underlying logic.

## II. Overlay Hierarchy (Fully Compatible Layered Structure)

| Layer | Content | Adaptation Notes |
|---|---|---|
| L0 | Base Model | The foundational character model. Facial features, physique, and standing pose are strictly locked and universal across both scenarios. |
| L1 | Makeup (Decision Layer) | Analyzes user cues to decide intensity/style: "Basic / Light / Formal / Cyber-Technical / Urban Commuter". Includes separate systems for traditional Chinese makeup and urban cyberpunk light-effect makeup. |
| L2 | Hairstyle | Chinese buns/updos/braids + traditional hair accessories or cyberpunk hairpieces. Includes both traditional and lightweight urban-cyber styles. High-precision hair strands are standard. |

| L5 | Accessories | Traditional hair/ear/neck/waist/hand accessories + Chinese cyberpunk tech/light components. Ancient scenes focus on traditional items with minor tech accents; urban scenes integrate both, with pure Western cyberpunk accessories strictly prohibited. |

> **Scope Boundary**: Character derivative assets consist only of L0–L5 (costume and styling). ## VIII. Character Portrait Series — Four-Angle Photography Specifications

> Even after applying styling, the output must remain a four-angle studio series to ensure consistency.

### View Definition

| Position | Angle | Shot Size | Photography Requirement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Left 1 | Front Close-up | Head to upper edge of clavicle | Face occupies 60%+, makeup details (foundation blending, eyeliner precision, lip texture) clearly visible. |
| Left 2 | Front 0° | Full Body | Front view of outfit, showing clothing drape, layering, and accessories. |
| Right 2 | Right Profile 90° | Full Body | Side profile, showing clothing layers and hairstyle from the side. |
| Right 1 | Back 180° | Full Body | Back of head hairstyle, back of outfit, back of bags/hats. |

### Visual Norms

| 项目 | 约束 |
| Item | Photography Requirement |
|---|---|
| Layout | Four angles side-by-side from left to right in the same frame, evenly spaced. |
| Background | Seamless medium gray backdrop paper #B0B0B0, no light spots, no gradients. |
| Posture | Maintain base model posture—asymmetric standing with shifted weight. No posing. |
| Expression | Micro-expression matching makeup and scene. |
| Lighting | Studio soft light—softbox main + reflectors. Soft shadows, light ratio 1:2 to 1:3. |
| Consistency | Same character, same styling, same shoot—all four angles are consistent. |
| Aspect Ratio | Recommended 4:1 or 16:4 wide aspect ratio. |

## I. Color Palette and Visual Tone
(Dual-scenario self-check: Clear implementation standards for both Ancient and Urban scenes, with no adaptation blind spots)

- **Color Foundation** — The entire piece utilizes Lunar White (C1), Cyan-Green (C2), and Indigo (C4) as the traditional oriental base colors, layered with core cyberpunk colors suited to the Chinese style (Vermilion neon, Fluorescent Cyan-Green, Cybernetic Indigo, and Gilded holographic tones). The overall color temperature is neutral-to-cool (4500-5800K). The saturation of traditional base colors is medium-to-high (55-75%), while the saturation of cyberpunk overlay colors can be controlled and heightened (70-90%). 【Ancient Scene】: Base colors are the core for architecture, costumes, and props. 【Urban Scene】: The same color family is used for skyscraper facades, cityscapes, public spaces, and costumes/props. Cyberpunk overlay colors are strictly anchored to the traditional oriental color system to avoid chaotic urban neon without oriental stylistic anchors.
- **Emotional Palette Drive** — Six sets of dual-scenario universal emotional palettes correspond to different narrative segments. Each set employs a "Traditional Base Layer + Cyberpunk Overlay Layer" dual-track system. Palette switching must be perfectly synchronized with the story arc:
  1. Palatial grandeur / Urban centers of power
  2. Landscape imagery / Urban sky gardens
  3. Gentle feminine chambers / Urban private residences
  4. Martial arts tension / Urban workplace rivalry / Crime investigation
  5. Festive celebration / Urban city festivals / Nightlife narratives
  6. Serene moonlit night / Urban night solitude / Empty city shots
- **Warm/Cool Narrative Contrast** — Traditional warm colors (Vermilion C3, Golden Yellow C5, Rouge C7, Gamboge C9) + Cyberpunk warm lighting (Neon Vermilion, Holographic Gilded) serve as visual signals for narrative shifts, used for emotional heating and festive segments. Traditional cool colors (Indigo C4, Ink Black C6) + Cyberpunk cool lighting (Fluorescent Cyan-Green, Cybernetic Indigo) are used for cold, melancholy, and serene segments. The light/color switching of cyberpunk elements serves as a visual anchor for strong narrative shifts. 【Ancient Scene】 focuses on traditional colors with cyberpunk as an accent; 【Urban Scene】 balances the weighting of the two tracks based on narrative needs.
- **Palette-First Principle** — Segment planning must first bind emotional scenarios, then determine the "Traditional Primary + Secondary + Cyberpunk Overlay" colors and lighting scheme. Avoid "correct plot, wrong emotion," ensuring the color logic of Ancient/Urban scenes is completely unified.
- **Prohibited Color Gamut** — High-saturation messy fluorescent colors, jarring modern digital colors, low-quality cheap neon, and illogical Western cyberpunk clashes are incompatible with this style. 【Urban Scene】 Additional prohibitions: street advertising colors without oriental anchors, chaotic street neon, and pure industrial cold grey tones.

## I. Positioning of the Storyboard Table
The storyboard is the director's core tool for translating a script into cinematic language. The following guidelines provide constraint specifications for the "Guofeng Cyber 3D" style. **The core principle is "Guofeng (Chinese style) at the core, Cybernetic form as an auxiliary."** Whether in classical historical settings or modern urban environments, the oriental classical elegance and narrative logic must be strictly maintained. **Cybernetic elements must not overshadow the core theme.**

---

## II. Universal Core Guidelines for Dual Settings (Mandatory for both Classical/Urban)
1.  **Style Ratio Law**: In every frame, core Guofeng elements (traditional colors, oriental forms, classical atmosphere, Chinese aesthetics) must account for ≥70%, while cybernetic technology elements must be ≤30%. Pure Western-style cyberpunk or soulless tech-stuffing is prohibited.
2.  **Dual-Setting Adaptation Logic**:
    - **Classical Settings**: Based on traditional Chinese architecture/attire/etiquette, with cybernetic elements acting as narrative embellishments that do not disrupt the integrity of the classical worldview.
    - **Modern Urban Settings**: Based on "New Chinese" (Xin Zhongshi) aesthetics and traditional oriental values. Cybernetic elements must be integrated with urban settings, incorporating grounded Guofeng symbols (New Chinese structural forms, traditional colors, oriental etiquette, traditional patterns). Pure urban cyberpunk without a Guofeng core is prohibited.
3.  **Unified Pacing Rule**: Regardless of setting, the film must maintain a steady, elegant oriental rhythm. Only strong narrative conflict segments may temporarily break this, and they must be clearly marked with specific durations and trigger logic.

---

## III. Lighting and Atmosphere (Fully adaptable to both Classical/Urban)
- **Unified Lighting within Scenes** — A single scene should not feature more than two lighting schemes unless there is a clear narrative pivot (e.g., day-to-night transitions, natural light changes like sun-to-rain, or shifts between reality and digital illusions). Cyber-specific lighting (neon, holograms, screen self-illumination, cybernetic glowing components) must be integrated into the scene's master lighting scheme. **Classical settings**: The color temperature of neon and electronic light must be consistent with traditional light sources (lanterns, candlelight, skylight). **Urban settings**: Cyber-lighting must be unified with basic Chinese light sources (New Chinese chandeliers, Guofeng street lamps, architectural outline lighting); stray or discordant light colors without narrative logic are prohibited.
- **Emotional Color Palette Binding** — Each scene must be tied to at least one emotional setting, applicable to both:
  - **Classical Basics**: Imperial Grandeur / Landscape Serenity / Boudoir Gentleness / Martial Arts Lethality / Festive Celebration / Moonlit Tranquility
  - **Guofeng Cyber Exclusives**: Cyber Martial-World Lethality / Digital Illusion Psychedelia / Cyber Imperial Power Struggle / Desolate Ancient Ruins / Bustling Neon Night Market / Guofeng Urban Workplace / Ancient Alley Mystery
  Maintain continuity of primary + secondary colors within a sequence. Rules: **Guofeng traditional colors must be the base.** Core palettes: Vermilion with Neon Cyan, Stone Cyan with Fluorescent Pink, Ink Black with Electronic Purple, Gilded Gold with Ice Blue. Never lose the Guofeng core.
- **3D Rendering Lighting Specifications** — Descriptions must specify volumetric light direction/intensity, ambient occlusion layers, and depth-of-field blur ranges. PBR material reflection/refraction is key to visual texture. 
  - **Classical**: Define PBR parameters for wood, brick, silk, and jade. Sync with holographic translucent volumetric light layers, neon pipeline light bleeding, and metal component reflections.
  - **Urban**: Define PBR parameters for glass curtain walls, concrete, and metal panels. Sync with light reflections of glass-etched Guofeng patterns, volumetric scattering of holographic ads, intensity of building outline lighting, and glow radii of cybernetic/wearable components. 
  All descriptions must clearly articulate the fusion logic between Guofeng materials and cyber materials to avoid disjointedness.
- **Tone Matching Narrative Stages** — Use cool tones (Indigo + Ink Black + low-saturation Neon Cyan) for lethal/melancholic/repressive/mysterious segments; avoid high-saturation warm tones like Vermilion/Gold. Warm tones (Vermilion + Gold + Rouge + low-saturation Neon Pink/Orange) are only for grand, gentle, or celebratory scenes. Glitch light and strobing are strictly reserved for narrative conflict (system crashes, memory flashbacks) with marked duration/frequency; **both are prohibited from misuse in either setting.**
- **Lighting Transitions as Advanced Techniques** — Lighting changes must be marked:
  - **Classical**: Soft window diffusion to moonlight = passage of time; Lantern warmth to holographic cool light = reality to digital illusion.
  - **Urban**: Street lamp warmth to office neon = transition from daily to work; Alley window light to skyscraper glass reflection = transition from traditional to modern. 
  Transitions must maintain a relaxed rhythm consistent with Guofeng elegance.

## IV. Environmental Dynamics (Fully adaptable to both)
- **Breathing Life into Scenes** — Every 3-4 shots, include one with environmental dynamics to prevent the screen from feeling "dead." **Mandatory requirement for both**: Every 3-4 shots must balance "Core Guofeng Dynamic Elements" with "Cyber Tech Dynamics."
  - **Classical**: Falling petals synced with holographic particle drift; candle flickering synced with screen-glitch micro-shimmer; swaying curtains synced with flowing hologram light; wind through bamboo synced with neon-pipeline breathing effects.
  - **Urban**: Swaying New Chinese awnings synced with under-eave neon flow; holographic calligraphy particles synced with falling leaves; architectural pattern reflections synced with indoor bonsai leaf movement; lantern breathing effects synced with passing train winds.
- **Prioritize Guofeng Environmental Elements** — Core dynamics must stem from the Guofeng worldview (Falling flowers, catkins, smoke, flowing water, wind, candles, curtains, ripples). Cyber dynamics are limited to: Holographic Guofeng patterns, neon breathing, digital-style brushwork/landscape flows, mechanical slow-motion, cybernetic micro-pulses, and subtle levitation of floating structures. **Universal Taboo**: Street graffiti, purely Western billboards, non-Guofeng modern cars, or Western cyber-machinery are prohibited.
- **3D Particles & Volumetric Effects** — Use 3D rendering to fuse effects.
  - **Classical**: Volumetric scattering of fog, PBR reflections on water, physical trajectories of falling flowers. Sync with Guofeng calligraphy holograms and holographic petal trails.
  - **Urban**: Volumetric scattering of rain/mist, PBR reflections of patterns on glass, physical trajectories of leaves. Sync with Guofeng landscape holograms and neon-rimmed Guofeng contours.
  All particle effects must define quantity, density, and speed to match the slow, elegant tempo. **Chaotic, high-speed particle movement is prohibited in both settings.**

## V. Movement Rhythm (Fully adaptable to both)
- **Core Rhythm: Slow, Stable, Restrained** — Characters' movements default to slow. Rising, turning, raising a hand must be labeled "slow." Cybernetic actions (holographic operation, mechanical expansion) must also be slow and elegant. **Only during intense conflict (fighting, crisis) can fast movements be marked, and they must not exceed 30% of the shot's duration.** Urban supplementary rule: Even in daily commute/office settings, maintain oriental poise. Avoid frantic movements, short steps, or exaggerated, flamboyant mechanical movements.
- **Attire Dynamics** — Leverage 3D fabric simulation for silk/cotton/linen drapes.
  - **Classical**: Mark the weight and drape of wide sleeves/hems. Sync with neon pipelines or glowing patterns on garments.
  - **Urban**: Mark the drape of New Chinese styles (modified cheongsams, etc.). Sync with glowing fasteners, mechanical cuffs, or holographic accessories.
  **Taboo**: Cyber components must not disrupt the core Guofeng silhouette.
- **Etiquette Constraints** — Movements must reflect oriental classical temperament (slow walking, subtle gestures). No modern body language (shrugging, palms up, leg shaking, large arm waves). **Urban supplementary**: Even cyber-related tasks (holographic panels, gate access, cyber-adjustments) must be done with subtle, precise, and graceful actions. Avoid mechanical coldness or stiff, jerky transitions.
- **3D Animation Texture** — Transitions must be natural and fluid. Ensure centers of gravity and joint arcs are natural. Bridge the transition between traditional limb movement and mechanical action seamlessly.

---

## VI. Camera Movement & Transition Taboos (Fully adaptable to both)
- **No Rapid Camera Movements** — Whip pans, sudden zooms, shaky-cam, high-speed tracking, strobing jumps, or fast first-person running are prohibited, as they clash with Guofeng 3D elegance. They are only allowed for extreme narrative conflict and must not exceed 20% of the shot's duration.
- **No Flashy Transitions** — Wipes, spins, shutters, white flashes, glitch-art artifacts, or zooming/bouncing are forbidden.
  - **Permitted**: Lighting gradients, element-matching (e.g., ancient eaves matching cyber eaves, jade pendant matching keycard, lantern matching street lamp), slow depth-tracking, or slow dissolves. All transitions must be slow and elegant.
- **Effective 3D Spatial Movement (Specifications)** — Use 3D spatial depth for slow pushes/wraps, maintaining a calm rhythm. Avoid "stunt" flying or穿越 (through-shot) movements.
  - **Classical**: Slow rotation around courtyards; slow push-ins revealing cyberized interiors; default speed ≤0.5m/s.
  - **Urban**: Slow push-in from an ancient-style alleyway gate toward skyscraper hubs; slow rotation around New Chinese office buildings to show the blend of glass and eaves; default speed ≤0.5m/s. All movement must serve narrative and mood, not effect-showcasing.
