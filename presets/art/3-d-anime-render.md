# 3D Animation Rendering Style Guide

This style is specifically designed for the "3D Animation Rendering" genre. All art prompts, specifications, and generated content are strictly limited to the following:

- **3D Animation Worldview**: Set in a modern urban context, covering streets, cafes, homes, offices, malls, etc., reflecting the atmosphere of modern city life.
- **Cel-shaded Texture**: All characters, scenes, and props must adhere to 3D animation rendering standards, emphasizing clear outlines, refined material textures, and layered light and shadow.
- **Healing Aesthetic**: Pursues a cinematic atmosphere based on 3D rendering, featuring warm and bright color tones, rich details, and an overall warm, healing, and lively ambiance.
- **Emotional Expression**: Suitable for showcasing gentle, healing, and romantic urban love stories, emphasizing emotional expression within modern urban aesthetics.

## Scope of Application

- AI art generation for 3D animation rendering themes (characters, scenes, props, storyboards, etc.)
- Visual creations requiring premium 3D rendering quality, delicate textures, and a warm, healing atmosphere.

## Prohibited Content

- Photorealistic rendering, photo-level realism
- Dark tones, heavy shadows, excessive contrast
- High-saturation fluorescent colors, neon colors
- Absence of modern elements, dark or violent content, adult-oriented content

## Style Experience

Under this style, you will experience:

- Characters with refined features and gentle or bright temperaments; clothing, makeup, and hairstyles are all in the 3D animation rendering style.
- Scene spaces with rich depth, clear and fluid outlines, and an atmosphere that is both realistic and poetic.
- Props, daily objects, and environmental details featuring modern designs, fine craftsmanship, and delicate textures.
- Storyboards and visuals based on 3D rendering, emphasizing narrative and emotional expression.

---

This style is only applicable to projects related to "3D Animation Rendering." If other styles (such as photorealistic 3D, CG, illustration, modern, etc.) are required, please do not use this set of prompts.

# 3D Animation Rendering Urban Character Derivative Asset Generation · Constraint Manual

---

## I. Overlay Principles

1.  **Facial Consistency** — After the overlay, facial features must be identical to the base model; facial shifting is strictly prohibited.
2.  **Postural Consistency** — Maintain the base model's natural standing posture; any changes to posture, action, or body language are prohibited.
3.  **Layer-wise Controllability** — Each layer is described independently to facilitate layer-by-layer replacement (change outfit without changing makeup).
4.  **Unified Style** — All apparel and styling elements must adhere to the same urban animation aesthetic system.
5.  **Quality Preservation** — The post-overlay quality must not be lower than the base model.
6.  **Pure Apparel/Styling Scope** — Only overlay makeup, hairstyles, clothing, and accessories; introducing props, scenes, environments, or actions is prohibited.

---

## II. Overlay Hierarchy

| Level | Content | Description |
|---|---|---|
| L0 | Base Model | Basic character model, do not modify |
| L1 | Makeup (Decision Layer) | Analyze user clues first, then decide intensity: "Basic / Light / Formal" |

| L3 | Undergarments | Replace basic white undergarment |
| L4 | Outerwear/Main Outfit | Modern urban clothing |

# 3D Animation Rendering Constraints · 3D Toon Shading · Technical Reference

---

## I. Color Palette and Visual Tone

- **Color Foundation** — The film utilizes Warm Orange (C1), Sky Blue (C3), and Cherry Blossom Pink (C5) as base colors. The overall color temperature is warm (4800-5200K) with medium-to-high saturation (65-80%), conveying the warm, healing aesthetic of bright 3D animation.
- **Narrative Contrast (Warm vs. Cold)** — Warm tones (Warm Orange C1, Amber, Warm Pink) permeate the film as the base tone, while cold tones (Pale Blue C3, Pale Purple C6) are used as selective accents to add depth. Switching between cold and warm tones should sync with the story arc rather than being mixed arbitrarily.
- **Warm/Cold Allocation Strategy** — Maintain a warm base throughout; cold tones should be reserved for nighttime or emotional turning points to create effective contrast.
- **Palette-First Principle** — When planning sequences, bind emotional settings (meeting/daily life/adventure/farewell) to specific palettes and lighting schemes first to avoid "the plot being right but the mood being color-mismatched."
- **Restricted Color Gamut** — Dull or grayish tones, overly saturated "dirty" colors, and any color systems that do not align with the bright, crisp quality of 3D animation are prohibited.

---

## II. Lighting Scheme System

- **Lighting as Narrative** — 6 lighting schemes correspond to different emotional sequences. The director should define the lighting tone at the sequence level during the planning stage, rather than specifying it shot-by-shot.
- **Light Angle** — Default to 45° side-backlighting to simulate the glow of a sunset.

| Lighting Scheme | Name | Color Tendency | Applicable Mood |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Soft Morning Light | Warm Yellow base + Uniform soft light | Opening sequence, urban morning |
| B | Warm Side Light | Warm Orange dominant + Local warm light | Daily warmth, cafe/home scenes |
| C | Top Light + Ambient | Neutral warm + Soft cool white | Work/study sequences |
| D | Backlit Sunset | Amber warm dominant + Warm pink accents | Sunset romance, sweet sequences |
| E | Neon Halo | Warm orange + Cool blue background | Night street scenes, romantic moments |
| F | Warm Soft Focus | Warm yellow dominant + Foggy effect | Memories/emotional sequences |

- **Cold/Warm Light Allocation** — Warm tones are used throughout; cold tones (Cool Blue + warm contrast) are used at night or after emotional shifts. Directors may adjust switching points based on narrative needs.
- **Atmospheric Mapping** — The atmospheric direction of every scene must map to one of the lighting schemes (A-F) to ensure visual consistency.

---

## III. Texture Direction

- **Celluloid Realism** — The core of 3D animation rendering: crisp outlines, bright coloring, and soft lighting layers.
- **High-Detail Materials + Soft Lighting** — This is the anchor of the visual texture. It is not about over-smoothing or modern photorealistic rendering; it is about the specific celluloid texture unique to 3D animation.
- **Rich Material Details** — All assets, costumes, and props must feature credible details: fabric folds, cartoon-style specular highlights on metal, and the warm sheen of food. "Hyper-realism" and "coarse, low-poly" looks are prohibited.
- **Celluloid Is Not Flat** — 3D animation emphasizes celluloid expression (clear outlines, bright coloring, soft lighting layers) to amplify impact through light, shadow, and compositional design, rather than relying on overly complex effects.

---

## IV. Urban Spatial Elements

Unique scene elements for the 3D animated urban worldview and their visual narrative functions:

- **Door/Window Frames, Corridors, Stairs** — Natural framing tools to create spatial depth and layering.
- **Streets, Parks, The Sky** — Natural carriers for "negative space" composition. Setting equals emotion: long shadows on a sunset street = romance; warm light in a cafe = security; window views of high-rises = urban detachment.
- **Desk Lamps, Neon, Window Light** — Light sources defining the urban world. Desk lamps = warm/intimate (Scheme B); neon = energetic/romantic (Scheme E); window light = daily/tranquil (Scheme A).
- **Use Empty Shots for Transitions** — This style possesses a rich library of scene assets (different times of day, weather variants). Sequence transitions should use empty environmental shots as emotional buffers instead of hard cuts.
- **Use Visuals + Action for Transitions** — Prioritize visual methods (lighting changes, scene shifts) combined with action design over relying on dialogue for exposition.

---

## V. Urban Musical Instruments and Ambient Sound

Constraints on sound elements for the 3D animated urban worldview:

### Instrument Selection

- **Piano** — The core instrument for daily life, warmth, and memory sequences; best captures the texture of 3D animation.
- **Guitar** — Suitable for relaxed, romantic, or street sequences; the gentleness of strumming fits the urban atmosphere.
- **Strings (Background/Bed)** — Used for emotional sublimation or farewells; the delicacy of bowed strings suits emotional transitions.
- **Electronic Sounds** — Best for youthful, rhythmic sequences; suitable for modern urban settings.
- Ambient sound is an important element but should not overshadow the music.

### Instrument Combination Strategy

| Emotional Stage | Instrument Combination |
|---|---|
| Stable/Opening/Ending | Solo Guitar or Electronic Sounds |
| Daily Warmth | Piano + Light Guitar |
| Emotional Sublimation/Romance | Strings + Piano |
| Tense/Urgent | Electronic Sounds + String Bed |

### Urban Ambient Sound

- **Typical Ambient Layers** — Urban traffic flow / Cafe background noise / Subway announcement pings / Wind in trees / Crowd chatter / Footsteps / Elevator sounds.
- **Mark 1-2 core ambient sounds for each scene** to assist in subsequent sound design. The richer the ambient layers, the more immersive the urban setting will be.

# Storyboard 3D Animation Rendering Constraints · 3D Toon Rendering · Technique Reference

---

## I. Storyboard Positioning

The storyboard is the core tool for a director to translate a script into cinematic language. The following guidelines provide specific constraints for the 3D toon rendering style at the storyboard level.

---

## II. Lighting and Atmosphere

- **Consistent Lighting per Scene** — Avoid using more than two lighting schemes within a single scene unless there is a clear narrative shift (e.g., turning off a desk lamp → cool moonlight).
- **Emotional Palette Binding** — Bind at least one emotional setting (e.g., romantic encounter/daily warmth/melancholic departure) to each scene and maintain continuity of primary and secondary colors across the shot sequence.
- **Light Source Angle Specification** — Default to 45° side-backlighting to simulate the glow of twilight. When describing lighting, explicitly distinguish between frontal and side-lit angles.
- **Matching Color Temperature to Narrative Stage** — Keep the first half (daily life/work/study) in warm tones; use cool tones (cool blue + warm contrast) at night or following an emotional turning point.
- **Lighting Transition as an Advanced Technique** — A gradient shift from natural light (A) to indoor warm light (B) represents the passage of time from day to night. Mark lighting change points in the storyboard.

---

## III. Environmental Dynamics

- **Environmental Dynamics for Visual Breathability** — Incorporate swaying leaves, drifting clouds, passing traffic, falling raindrops, or flickering lights. Schedule at least one shot with environmental motion every 3-4 shots to ensure the frame does not feel "stagnant."
- **Prioritize Urban Environmental Elements** — Environmental dynamics should utilize elements consistent with the urban setting: swaying leaves, drifting clouds, passing traffic, neon flickering, and swaying curtains. Elements incompatible with the 3D animation style are prohibited.

---

## IV. 3D Animation Action Pacing

- **Natural Motion** — All character movements default to a natural pace. Standing up, turning, or raising a hand should be labeled as "natural" or "gentle."
- **Use Subtlety for Sweet Interactions** — Focus on eye contact, slight finger brushes, or fingertips touching while passing objects. Do not script large-scale dual-character interactions in the storyboard; break them down into suggestive, localized shots.
- **3D Animation Fluidity** — Emphasize the "inter-frame fluidity" characteristic of 3D animation. Actions should be coherent and natural to avoid stiff or jarring frame skipping.

---

## V. Camera Movement Prohibitions

- **Prohibit Rapid Camera Movements** — Whip pans, rapid zooms, and fast cuts conflict with the aesthetic of 3D toon rendering (unless used specifically for transitions or emphasis).
- **Prohibit Fancy Transitions** — Wipes, spins, blinds, and other similar transitions are incompatible with this style.
