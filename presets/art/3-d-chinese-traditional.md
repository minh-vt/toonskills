# Guofeng 3D Style Guide

This style is specifically tailored for the "Guofeng (Chinese Style) 3D" theme. All art prompts, specifications, and generated content are strictly limited to the following:

- **Guofeng 3D Rendering**: Presents traditional Chinese aesthetics using 3D modeling and rendering techniques, covering ancient architecture, landscapes and gardens, imperial attire, etc.
- **3D Refined Texture**: High-precision models, PBR material rendering, rich light and shadow layers, and clearly discernible details.
- **Traditional Chinese Color Palette**: Traditional palettes such as cyan-green, vermilion, indigo, and golden yellow, featuring harmonious and elegant colors.
- **Oriental Aesthetic Atmosphere**: Profound artistic conception, interplay between void and reality, negative space composition, and poetic space.
- **Ancient-style Character Design**: 3D ancient-style modeling, magnificent and exquisite clothing, elegant makeup, and sophisticated hairstyles.
- **Cinematic Lighting**: Cinematic rendering effects such as volumetric lighting, ambient occlusion, global illumination, and depth-of-field blurring.

## Scope of Application

- AI art generation for Guofeng 3D themes (characters, scenes, props, storyboards, etc.)
- Visual creations requiring 3D rendering textures, Oriental aesthetics, and ancient-style artistic conception.
- Application scenarios such as game art, film concept design, and Guofeng animation.

## Prohibited Content

- Photorealistic/Photo-level realism (unless specifically stated)
- Western fantasy/Gothic/Victorian styles
- Cyberpunk/Sci-fi/Modern urban elements
- Low-precision models/Rough modeling/Blurred textures
- Over-saturation/Fluorescent colors/Strong digital "feel"

## Style Experience

Under this style, you will experience:

- Elegant and exquisite character designs with delicate 3D modeling, clear clothing textures, and flowing hair.
- Magnificent scenes with rich architectural details, realistic material textures, and poetic lighting atmospheres.
- Sophisticated props and artifacts with fine craftsmanship and harmonious color schemes.
- Storyboarding and rendering that emphasize 3D texture, Oriental artistic conception, and cinematic lighting.

# Character Derivative Asset Generation · Constraint Manual

---

## I. Overlay Principles

1. **Face Consistency** — After overlaying, facial features must be identical to the base model; facial shifting is strictly prohibited.
2. **Pose Consistency** — Maintain the base model's natural standing posture; any changes to pose, motion, or body stance are prohibited.
3. **Layer-by-Layer Control** — Each layer is described independently to facilitate layer-specific replacement (e.g., changing clothing without changing makeup).
4. **Unified Style** — All fashion and styling elements must conform to the same aesthetic system.
5. **No Quality Degradation** — The quality standard after overlaying must not be lower than the base model.
6. **Fashion & Styling Only** — Strictly limited to makeup, hairstyles, clothing, and accessories; introducing props, scenes, environments, or actions is prohibited.

---

## II. Overlay Hierarchy

| Level | Content | Description |
|---|---|---|
| L0 | Base Model | Base image, no modifications allowed. |
| L1 | Makeup (Decision Layer) | Analyze user cues first, then decide on intensity: "Basic / Light / Formal." |
| L2 | Hairstyle & Styling | Buns / Updos / Braids + hair accessories. |
| L3 | Undergarments | Replace basic white undergarments. |
| L4 | Outerwear / Primary Costume | Traditional robes / ceremonial attire / casual wear. |
| L5 | Accessories | Headpieces / earrings / necklaces / waist ornaments / hand jewelry. |

> **Scope Boundaries**: Character derivative assets include only layers L0–L5 (fashion and styling). They exclude props (handheld items like umbrellas, swords, fans, books, lanterns), environmental scenes (indoors/outdoors/weather), and postures/actions (walking/turning head/raising hands). These fall under different asset categories.

---

## III. Makeup Constraints (L1)

### Strategy: From Base Model to Derivative Makeup (Critical)

> Although the base character model is bare-faced, derivative assets must enter the makeup workflow by default. The system should analyze user cues to decide intensity (Basic, Light, or Formal) instead of retaining the bare-faced look.

### L1 Cue Analysis and Makeup Decision

| Step | Processing Content | Decision Result |
|---|---|---|
| S1 | Extract user cues: facial state words, emotional words, intensity words. | Formulate makeup requirement summary. |
| S2 | Filter non-makeup cues: props/scenes/actions/posture words are not used as a basis for makeup. | Prevent misjudgment. |
| S3 | Match makeup style matrix and assign intensity level. | Basic / Light / Formal. |
| S4 | Generate final L1 prompt. | Output only the conclusion, not the analysis process. |

### Mapping Cues to Makeup (Execution Standards)

| Cue Type | Typical Cues | L1 Decision |
|---|---|---|
| No obvious facial cues | Only clothing/hair changes; no emphasized emotion or state. | Basic Makeup |
| Minor facial cues | Soft, smiling, fluttering eyelashes, slightly improved complexion. | Light Makeup (Very subtle) |
| Clear daily cues | Daily, outing, casual. | Basic Makeup (Natural & clear) |
| Clear formal ceremony cues | Wedding, ceremony, important occasion. | Formal Makeup (Exquisite & grand) |

> Decision Principle: All derivative assets must have makeup applied. Determine intensity and style based on facial cues; changes in props, scenes, or postures must not independently raise makeup intensity.

# Traditional Chinese 3D Constraints · Traditional Chinese 3D · Technique Reference

---

## I. Color System and Visual Tone

- **Color Foundation** — The film is grounded in Moon White (C1), Cyan-Green (C2), and Indigo Blue (C4). The overall color temperature is neutral (4800-5500K) with medium-high saturation (55-75%), reflecting the elegant and grand aesthetic of traditional Oriental art.
- **Emotional Palette Driven** — Six emotional palettes (Palatial Grandeur / Landscapes and Mood / Gentle Boudoir / Martial Arts and Lethality / Festive Celebration / Quiet Moonlight) correspond to different narrative segments. Switching palettes must synchronize with the story arc.
- **Contrast of Cold and Warm Narratives** — Warm colors (Vermilion C3, Golden Yellow C5, Rouge C7, Gamboge C9) serve as visual signals for narrative shifts, used for emotional escalation and celebratory scenes. Cold colors (Indigo Blue C4, Ink Black C6) are used for scenes of lethality, melancholy, or tranquility.
- **Palette-First Principle** — When planning segments, bind them to an emotional scene type (Palatial Grandeur, Landscapes and Mood, etc.) first, then define the primary/secondary colors and lighting scheme. Avoid "correct plot, wrong emotional color."
- **Restricted Color Gamut** — High-saturation fluorescent colors, neon colors, and modern digital color systems are incompatible with this style.

---

## II. Lighting Scheme System

- **Lighting as Narrative** — 7 lighting schemes correspond to different emotional segments. During the directorial planning phase, establish the tonal direction of the lighting at the segment level rather than specifying it shot-by-shot.
- **3D Render Lighting Characteristics** — Volumetric lighting, Ambient Occlusion (AO), and Depth of Field (DoF) blurring are the core lighting tools for the Traditional Chinese 3D style. All schemes must reflect PBR (Physically Based Rendering) material quality.

| Lighting Scheme | Name | Tone Tendency | Applicable Emotion |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Warm Splendor | Vermilion + Golden Yellow Highlights + Moon White Base | Palatial Grandeur, Dignity, Grand Celebration |
| B | Cyan-Green Mood | Cyan-Green + Moon White Mist + Volumetric Diffusion | Landscapes, Poetic Distance, Ethereal |
| C | Soft Warm Shadows | Rouge Warmth + Golden Accents + Soft Shadows | Gentle Boudoir, Delicate Beauty, Daily Comfort |
| D | Cold Lethality | Ink Black + Indigo + High Contrast Hard Light | Martial Arts, Sharpness, Oppressive Atmosphere |
| E | Window Diffused Light | Moon White Base + Natural Side Light + AO | Indoor Day, Daily Life, Quiet Elegance |
| F | Moonlight Radiance | Indigo + Moon White Cold Light + Golden Warm Accents | Quiet Moonlight, Serenity, Solitary Longing |
| G | Festive Warmth | Vermilion + Gamboge + High Saturation Warm Light | Festivals, Lively Joy, Vibrant Colors |

- **Cold/Warm Light Distribution** — Warm lights (Vermilion/Golden Yellow/Gamboge) apply to grand, gentle, or festive scenes. Cold lights (Indigo/Ink Black) apply to lethal, melancholy, or quiet scenes. Directors may adjust cold-warm transition points based on narrative needs.
- **Atmospheric Mapping** — Every scene's atmosphere should map to one of the above lighting schemes (A-G) to ensure visual consistency.

---

## III. Texture Direction

- **3D Rendering as Anchor** — The core of Traditional Chinese 3D: High-precision modeling, PBR texture rendering, volumetric light, ambient occlusion, and DoF blurring to achieve cinematic 3D visuals.
- **PBR Materials First** — All props and assets must have credible PBR physics: the luster and drape of silk, the grain and patina of wood, the reflection and texture of metal, the translucence and warmth of jade, and the glaze of porcelain.
- **Volumetric Light and Depth of Field** — Volumetric light is the soul of this 3D style. Outdoor scenes must have aerial perspective and volumetric scattering; indoor scenes must create volumetric effects via window/candle light. DoF reinforces spatial depth.
- **Aged Texture** — Materials should not look overly clean or perfect: wooden surfaces should show signs of use, stone should have weathering and moss, fabrics should have natural folds, and tiles should show moss or chips. "Plastic-like" or flawless "CG-look" is strictly prohibited.
- **3D Does Not Mean Cold** — This style emphasizes the "warmth" of Oriental aesthetics. Convey emotion through material texture, lighting layers, and color combinations rather than relying on spectacular effects.

---

## IV. Traditional Scene Space Elements

Unique scene elements in the ancient world and their visual narrative functions:

- **Sheer Curtains/Screens/Door Frames** — Natural framing tools to create depth and a sense of "obscurity." In 3D rendering, the translucent material and light transmission of sheer curtains are highlights.
- **Courtyards/Flowering Trees/Rain** — Natural carriers for "negative space" composition. Scene = Emotion: Garden in full bloom = Release; Sitting alone in rain = Loneliness; Falling leaves = Parting sorrow. The volume of vegetation and light interaction is crucial.
- **Candlelight/Moonlight/Window Light** — Light sources of the ancient world. Candlelight = Warm/Intimate (Scheme C); Moonlight = Cold/Quiet (Scheme F); Window light = Daily/Stillness (Scheme E). Volumetric light and PBR reflections are key.
- **Eaves/Brackets/Blue Tiles** — Iconic architectural elements. 3D modeling must reflect detailed craftsmanship, and materials must show the passage of time.
- **Use Empty Shots for Transitions** — This style has rich scene assets (variations in time/weather/seasons). Use empty shots as emotional buffers between segments rather than hard cuts.
- **Use Visuals, Not Dialogue, for Transitions** — Prioritize visual methods (lighting shifts, jump cuts, metaphorical empty shots) over dialogue-based explanations.

---

## V. Ancient Instruments and Ambient Sound

Constraints on sound elements for the ancient worldview:

### Instrument Selection

- **Xiao (Flute)** — Core instrument for bleak, lonely, and sorrowful segments. Best for conveying cold, melancholic moods.
- **Erhu** — Used for stirring emotions, grief, and longing. The "crying" sound of the bow is perfect for emotional outbursts.
- **Suona** — Used for scenes with intense emotional fluctuation (great joy/grief, fate shifts, climaxes). Use with caution—it is highly impactful.
- **Guqin** — For setting the tone at the start or steady segments. Used with Xiao to reflect landscape moods.
- **Pipa** — Accents for tense or hurried scenes, suitable for martial arts/lethal atmosphere.
- **Guzheng** — Atmosphere instrument for palatial grandeur or festive celebrations; elegant and magnificent.
- *String backings can add cinematic quality but should not overpower the main instruments.*

### Instrument Combination Strategy

| Emotional Stage | Instrument Combination |
|---|---|
| Steady/Opening/Closing | Guqin Solo or Guqin + Xiao |
| Landscape/Ethereal | Xiao + Guqin + Dizi (Bamboo Flute) |
| Palatial/Festive | Guzheng + Chimes + Strings |
| Growing Sorrow | Xiao + Erhu |
| Emotional Outburst/Fate Shift | Suona Solo or Suona + Erhu |
| Martial/Tense | Pipa Accents + String Bedding |
| Gentle/Daily Life | Guqin + Dizi + Light Strings |

### Ancient Ambient Sound

- **Typical Layers** — Cicada chirping / Babbling brooks / Wind through bamboo forests / Marketplace bustle / Night rain on eaves / Fabric rustling / Wind chimes / Birdsong / Falling petals.
- **Annotate 1-2 core ambient sounds for every scene** to assist in post-production sound design. The richer the ambient layers, the more immersive the traditional scene becomes.

# Storyboard Table Chinese-Style 3D Constraints · Chinese-Style 3D · Technique Reference

---

## I. Storyboard Positioning

The storyboard is the core tool for the director to translate a script into lens language. The following guidelines specifically outline the constraints for the Chinese-style 3D aesthetic at the storyboard level.

---

## II. Lighting and Atmosphere

- **Unified Lighting within a Scene** — There should not be more than two lighting schemes within a single scene unless there is a clear narrative pivot (e.g., natural light changes like day-to-night or sunny-to-rainy).
- **Emotional Color Palette Binding** — Each scene must be bound to at least one emotional setting (e.g., Imperial Opulence / Landscape Poetics / Gentle Boudoir / Martial Arts Lethality / Festive Celebration / Serene Moonlight), maintaining consistency in primary and secondary colors within a sequence of shots.
- **3D Rendering Lighting Specifications** — When describing lighting, clearly specify the direction and intensity of volumetric light, the levels of ambient occlusion, and the range of depth-of-field blurring. The reflection/refraction effects of PBR materials under different lighting are critical to the visual texture.
- **Matching Cold/Warm Tones with Narrative Stages** — Use cold tones (indigo + ink black) for lethal, melancholy, or oppressive segments; avoid warm tones like vermilion or golden yellow. Warm tones (vermilion + golden yellow + rouge) should only be used in opulent, gentle, or festive segments.
- **Lighting Transitions as Advanced Techniques** — A gradient from diffused window light (E) to moonlight radiance (F) denotes the passage of time from day to night. Mark lighting change points clearly in the storyboard.

---

## III. Environmental Dynamics

- **Environmental Dynamics for Visual Breathing Room** — Include elements such as falling petals, rising smoke, rippling water, billowing curtains, or dust particles in volumetric light. Arrange at least one shot with environmental dynamics for every 3–4 shots to prevent a "dead" visual.
- **Priority of Classical Elements** — Environmental dynamics must utilize elements from a classical world-view: falling flowers, willow catkins, light smoke, flowing water, wind through bamboo groves, or flickering candles. Modern elements are strictly prohibited.
- **3D Particles and Volumetric Effects** — Chinese-style 3D environmental dynamics should fully leverage the advantages of 3D rendering: volumetric light scattering in fog, PBR reflections on water surfaces, physics-based trajectories for falling petals, and volumetric smoke rendering.

---

## IV. Classical Action Pacing

- **Classical Actions Should Be Slow** — All character movements default to a slow pace. Actions such as standing up, turning, or raising a hand should be noted as "slow."
- **Garment Dynamics** — The movement of classical attire (wide sleeves, flowing skirts, ribbons) is a natural dynamic asset. The drape and floating effects of fabric simulations in 3D rendering should be reflected in the visual description to enhance the "liveliness" of the shot.
- **Etiquette Constraints** — Classical character demeanor must align with the period's temperament: steady and slow walking, subtle and restrained gestures; modern body language (e.g., shrugging, throwing hands up) is prohibited.
- **3D Animation Quality** — Transitions between actions should be natural and smooth, reflecting the sophistication of 3D animation. Avoid stiff pose switching; pay attention to natural joint arcs and shifts in center of gravity.

---

## V. Camera Movement Prohibitions

- **No Rapid Camera Movements** — Whip pans, rapid zooms, and handheld shakes conflict with the elegant temperament of the Chinese 3D style.
- **No Fancy Transitions** — Wipes, rotations, blinds, etc., are incompatible with this style.
- **Leverage 3D Spatial Movement** — Utilize the spatial depth of 3D scenes for slow dollies or orbits, but maintain a steady and elegant rhythm. Flamboyant, showy camera maneuvers like "fly-throughs" are prohibited.
