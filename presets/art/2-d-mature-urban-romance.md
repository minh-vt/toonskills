# Style Specification for Mature Urban Romance Anime

This style is specifically designed for **mature urban romance anime**. All art prompts, specifications, and generated content are strictly limited to the following:

- **Low-Saturation Cool Tones**: The overall color palette leans toward cool tones, with warm colors used only as accents to highlight a sweet, romantic atmosphere that is both sophisticated and gentle.

- AI art generation for mature urban romance themes (characters, scenes, props, storyboards, etc.)
- Visual creation for anime adaptations of modern novels
- Visual content requiring clean lines, cel shading, and cinematic composition

- Photorealistic/Live-action/3D render styles
- High-saturation fluorescent/neon colors

1. **Face as the Soul** — The facial features are the character's primary anchor point, with detail levels adapted to an anime style.

# Urban Romance Constraints · Sophisticated Urban Romance 2D Anime · Technique Reference

---

## I. Color Palette and Visual Tone

- **Color Foundation** — The entire film is based on cool white skin (C1), light blue (C3), and cyan-gray (C6). The overall color temperature is cool (5800-7000K) with low-to-medium saturation (30-50%), presenting a crisp, restrained, and premium gray tone.
- **Cool vs. Warm Narrative Contrast** — Warm colors (Amber Warm C7, Mist Pink C5) serve as visual signals for narrative shifts, used for local accents during moments of emotional warming and romantic tension. The transition between cool and warm should synchronize with the story arc rather than being mixed arbitrarily.
- **Cool/Warm Allocation Strategy** — Segments involving separation, repression, or indifference must strictly maintain the cool base (Cyan-gray C6 + Light blue C3), disabling warm tones like Amber or Mist Pink. Warm colors should only be released after emotional reconciliation, using the contrast as a narrative pivot.
- **Palette-First Principle** — Segment planning must first bind emotional scenarios (first heartbeats, romantic tension, protective promises, separation misunderstandings, reunion relief, or night banquets, etc.) before determining the primary/secondary colors and lighting schemes to avoid "a plot that works but emotions that don't match the colors."
- **Forbidden Gamuts** — High-saturation fluorescent colors, neon colors, and modern digital color systems are incompatible with this style.

---

## II. Lighting Scheme System

- **Lighting as Narrative** — 7 lighting schemes correspond to different emotional segments. During the director's planning phase, the tonal direction of the lighting should be determined at the segment level, rather than being specified shot-by-shot.
- **Cel-Shading Lighting Characteristics** — Clear light-dark boundaries and dramatic low-key lighting are the core methods. Lighting must serve emotional narration while maintaining the cel-shading style of 2D animation.

| Lighting Scheme | Scheme Name | Color Tendency | Applicable Emotion |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Soft Side/Backlight | Cool white base + local warm silhouette contouring | Heartbeat, tender affection, daily warmth |
| B | Hard High-Contrast | Ink black C4 + Silver gray C8 hard light split | Confrontation, cold anger, tense opposition |
| C | Diffused Cool Light | Cyan-gray C6 + Light blue C3 low-key lighting | Repressed sadness, separation/misunderstanding, loneliness |
| D | Diffused Warm Light | Amber warm C7 + Mist pink C5 (local) | Romantic tension, reunion relief, intimacy |
| E | Natural Window Light | Plain white C10 + Light blue C3 mottled side light | Indoor daytime, daily life, work commute |
| F | Night City Lights | Amber warm C7 accents + Cyan-gray C6 dark areas | Night banquets, city night scenes, romantic light |
| G | Cold Moonlight | Light blue C3 + Silver gray C8 cold light | Nighttime loneliness, longing, detachment |

- **Cool/Warm Light Allocation** — Warm light (Amber Warm C7) is suitable for emotional recovery and intimate/romantic segments; cool light (Cyan-gray C6 + Light blue C3) is for separation, repression, or indifference. Directors may adjust the switching points based on narrative needs.
- **Atmospheric Mapping** — The atmospheric direction of every scene should map to one of the above lighting schemes (A-G) to ensure visual consistency.

---

## III. Texture Direction

- **Cel-Shading as the Anchor** — The core of sophisticated urban romance 2D anime: clear lines, cel-shaded coloring, and dramatic low-key lighting, presenting a cinematic 2D animation look.
- **Face and Skin** — Refined facial rendering, delicate skin, and exquisite features. Female characters have transparent but not pale cold-white skin (C1); male characters have fresh and natural warm-white skin (C2). Skin should maintain the smooth, delicate texture characteristic of cel-shading.
- **Clothing Material** — Clear fabric textures, hyper-clear detail, realistic cloth folds, and fluid movement. The texture of modern urban attire (suits/shirts/dresses/coats) must look authentic.
- **Scene Material** — Crisp material textures, aerial perspective, signs of modern use, and lived-in atmosphere. Glass reflections, metallic luster, wood grains, and fabric drapes must all be clearly presented within the cel-shading style.
- **Cel-Shading Is Not Flat** — Sophisticated urban romance emphasizes layering cinematic lighting and depth-of-field blurring on top of cel-shading, amplifying emotional impact through composition and design rather than relying on photorealistic accumulation.

---

## IV. Urban Spatial Elements

Unique scene elements and their visual narrative functions in a modern urban setting:

- **Floor-to-Ceiling Windows/Glass Partitions/Door Frames** — Natural framing tools that create "impenetrable" layers and spatial depth. Glass reflections and refractions are highlights.
- **City Skyline/Streets/Parks** — Natural vehicles for negative space composition; the scene is the emotion: busy street scenes = noisy loneliness; sitting alone on a bench in the rain = isolation; leaves drifting across the street = lingering sorrow.
- **Lighting/Window Light/Screen Light** — Sources of light in the modern city. Indoor warm lights = warmth/intimacy (Scheme D); city night scenes = intertwining of cool and warm (Scheme F); natural window light = daily/serene (Scheme E).
- **Use Empty Shots for Transitions** — This style features rich scene assets (variations in time/weather). It is recommended to use empty shots as emotional buffers between segments rather than hard cuts. City skylines, rain outside windows, or empty cafes can serve as transitional empty shots.
- **Use Visuals, Not Dialogue, for Turning Points** — Prioritize visual methods (sudden changes in lighting, jump cuts in shot size, metaphorical empty shots) rather than relying on dialogue for explanation.

---

## V. Urban Scoring and Ambient Sound

Constraints on audio elements in an urban romance worldview:

### Musical Instrument Selection

- **Piano** — The core instrument, with the widest application: solos for isolation/longing/memory segments, light and quick keys for daily/heartbeat segments.
- **Strings (Violin/Cello/String Ensemble)** — Used for emotional layering and elevation. Cello is low and deep, suitable for repression/restraint; violin is high-pitched, suitable for heartbeats/thrills; string ensembles are suitable for emotional climaxes.
- **Guitar (Acoustic/Electric)** — Acoustic guitar for daily warmth/relaxed segments; clean electric guitar for urban night scenes/tipsy atmospheres.
- **Electronic Ambient Pads** — Low-frequency electronic pads used as a bed for city empty shots/lonely segments to enhance modern urban spatial sensation.
- **Flute/Clarinet** — Accents for gentle segments; not used as leads, but for atmospheric support in mornings/parks/daily life.

# Storyboard Constraints for Urban Romance · Mature Anime Style · Technical Reference

## I. Storyboard Positioning

The storyboard is the director's core tool for translating a script into cinematic language. The following guidelines provide specific constraints for the storyboard table to maintain a mature urban romance anime style.

---

## II. Lighting and Atmosphere

- **Unified Lighting within Scenes** — A single scene should not feature more than two lighting schemes, unless there is a clear narrative shift (e.g., switching indoor lights → twilight deepening outside, or sunny weather → sudden light rain).
- **Emotional Palette Binding** — Each scene must be tied to at least one emotional setting (e.g., love at first sight/ambiguous tension/promises of protection/separation and misunderstanding/reunion and relief/night banquets), maintaining consistency of primary and secondary colors throughout the shot sequence.
- **Light Source Angle Specifications** — Soft side-backlighting or diffused light is the default for head-on shots; adjust side-angle shots as required by the composition. When describing lighting, distinguish between natural light (window light/sunlight) and artificial light (lamps/screen glow).
- **Matching Color Temperature to Narrative Stages** — Use cool color palettes (Cyan-Grey C6 + Light Blue C3) strictly for separation, depression, or indifference; warm tones are prohibited. Warm tones (Amber Warm C7 + Haze Pink C5) are reserved exclusively for segments featuring emotional reconciliation or growing romantic tension.
- **Lighting as a Sophisticated Transition** — Transitions from natural outdoor light to warm indoor lighting signify the passage of time (day to night); transitions from a bright office to a dimly lit apartment signify emotional shifts. Clearly mark these light transition points in the storyboard.

---

## III. Environmental Dynamics

- **Environmental Dynamics for "Breathing" Scenes** — Incorporate elements like swaying curtains, rising steam from coffee cups, raindrops sliding down glass, urban lights flickering, or falling leaves. Include at least one shot with environmental motion every 3-4 shots to prevent the imagery from feeling "static."
- **Prioritize Modern Urban Elements** — Environmental dynamics must reflect a modern urban worldview: swaying curtains, flowing car light trails, rain streaks on glass, leaves drifting across streets, rising coffee steam, or flickering streetlamp halos. Ancient/fantasy elements are prohibited.
- **Cel-Shading Dynamics** — Environmental movement should align with anime conventions: hand-drawn textures of dappled light, the aesthetic lines of fluttering fabric, and cel-shaded rendering effects for rain streaks and lighting.

---

## IV. Pacing of Urban Character Actions

- **Restrained Action** — Actions for mature urban romance characters should be subtle and contained; emotions should not be overt. Mark key actions (turning around, raising a hand, glancing back) as "slow" or "composed."
- **Clothing Dynamics** — Micro-movements of modern urban attire (suit tails, skirt hems, scarves, coat flaps) are natural assets. Include descriptions of slight movement of clothing due to wind or action to add a sense of "life" to the frame.
- **Posture Constraints** — Gestures must reflect mature, elite refinement: standing tall and composed, moving with grace and restraint. Avoid exaggerated anime-style body language (e.g., over-the-top gestures or chibi-style deformations).
- **Micro-Expressions Prioritized** — Convey emotions through facial micro-expressions (the curve of the lips, a subtle twitch of the eyebrow, a shift in gaze) rather than relying on broad physical movements.

---

## V. Camera Movement Prohibitions

- **Prohibit Fast Camera Movement** — Whip pans, rapid zooms, and handheld shaking are strictly forbidden, as they clash with the cool, elegant atmosphere of mature urban romance anime.
- **Prohibit Flashy Transitions** — Wipes, spins, blinds, white flashes, etc., are incompatible with this style.
- **Effective Use of Depth of Field and Space** — Utilize the spatial depth of modern urban settings for slow push-ins/pull-outs or tracking shots. Use shallow depth of field to create a cinematic feel, while maintaining a steady and restrained tempo.
