# Guofeng Anime "New Guochao" Style Guide

This style is specifically designed for the "Guofeng (National Style) Anime New Guochao" genre. All art prompts, specifications, and generated content are strictly limited to the following:

- **Guofeng Anime Style**: 2D animation style set against a backdrop of traditional Chinese culture, covering themes such as Xianxia (fantasy hero), Wuxia (martial arts), and historical court life.
- **New Guochao Aesthetics**: A fusion of traditional Oriental elements with modern sensibilities, balancing classical charm with contemporary fashion.
- **Japanese Animation Rendering Techniques**: Modern Japanese animation coloring and lighting techniques, combining cel-shading with digital rendering.
- **Oriental Classical Charm**: Poetic atmosphere, "negative space" (liubai) composition, and profound artistic expression rooted in Oriental aesthetics.
- **Refined Brushwork**: Clear lines, intricate details, and exquisite character and scene design.
- **Guofeng Anime Cinematic Quality**: Cinematic composition, layered lighting and shadow, dynamic tension, and emotional atmosphere.

## Scope of Application

- AI art generation for Guofeng anime themes (characters, scenes, props, storyboards, etc.).
- Visual creations requiring New Guochao aesthetics, Japanese animation rendering, and Guofeng artistic atmosphere.
- Guofeng animation, game art, conceptual design, and illustration creation.

## Strictly Prohibited Content

- Photorealistic photography/3D realistic rendering.
- Western fantasy/Gothic/Victorian styles.
- Cyberpunk/Sci-fi/Excessively modern elements.
- Poor line quality/blurred image quality/low-precision modeling.
- Chaotic color palettes/inconsistent styles/aesthetic dissonance.

## Style Experience

Under this style, you will experience:

- Characters with anime-style proportions, exquisite traditional attire, and smooth, refined lines.
- Scenes filled with Oriental artistic conception, rich in traditional architectural details, with refined lighting and shadow rendering.
- Sophisticated props and artifacts with clear material textures and harmonious, unified color palettes.
- Storyboards and renderings that emphasize cinematic quality, New Guochao aesthetics, and Japanese animation techniques.

# Derivative Character Asset Generation · Constraint Manual

---

## I. Superposition Principles

1. **Facial Consistency** — After superposition, facial features must match the base model exactly; facial deviation is prohibited.
2. **Static Pose** — Maintain the base model's natural standing posture; any changes in pose, action, or body posture are prohibited.
3. **Layered Control** — Describe each layer independently to facilitate layer-by-layer replacement (e.g., change outfit without changing makeup).
4. **Stylistic Uniformity** — All attire and grooming elements must conform to the same aesthetic system.
5. **Quality Maintenance** — Post-superposition quality must not be lower than the base model.
6. **Pure Attire/Grooming Scope** — Only apply makeup, hairstyles, outfits, and accessories; introducing props, scenes, environments, or actions is prohibited.

---

## II. Superposition Layers

| Layer | Content | Description |
|---|---|---|
| L0 | Base Model | Foundation image model, do not modify |
| L1 | Makeup (Decision Layer) | Analyze user clues first, then decide on intensity: "Basic / Light / Formal" |
| L2 | Hairstyle | Hair bun / bound hair / braided hair + hair accessories |
| L3 | Undergarment | Replacement of white base inner layers |
| L4 | Outerwear/Main Outfit | Traditional Chinese-style attire / robes / daily wear |
| L5 | Accessories | Headwear / earrings / necklaces / waist ornaments / wrist jewelry |

> **Scope Boundaries**: Derivative character assets only include L0–L5 layers (attire, grooming, and styling). These fall under other asset categories.

## III. Makeup Constraints (L1)

### Strategy from Base Model to Derivative Makeup (Key)

> Although the character base model may appear bare-faced, derivative assets default to the grooming workflow. The system must analyze the requirements based on user-provided clues and decide on the intensity between Basic, Light, or Formal makeup, rather than keeping a bare-faced look.

### L1 Clue Analysis and Makeup Decision

| Step | Processing Content | Decision Result |
|---|---|---|
| S1 | Extract user clues: facial state words, emotional words, intensity words | Formulate makeup requirement summary |
| S2 | Filter non-makeup clues: props/scenes/actions/posture words are not used as a basis for makeup | Prevent misjudgment |
| S3 | Match with makeup style matrix and assign intensity level | Basic / Light / Formal |
| S4 | Generate final L1 prompt | Output conclusion only, no analysis process |

### Clue-to-Makeup Mapping (Execution Standards)

| Clue Type | Typical Clues | L1 Decision |
|---|---|---|
| No specific facial emphasis | Outfit/hair changes only, no emphasis on emotion/state | Basic Makeup |
| Slight facial clues | Soft, smiling, fluttering eyelashes, subtle complexion enhancement | Light Makeup (very faint) |
| Explicit daily clues | Daily, going out, casual | Basic Makeup (natural/clear) |
| Explicit formal/ritual clues | Grand wedding, ceremony, important occasion | Formal Makeup (exquisite/glamorous) |

> Determination Principle: All derivative assets must include grooming. Use facial clues to determine intensity and style; changes in props, scenes, or poses shall not independently increase makeup intensity.

# Traditional-Style Anime Constraint · New Traditional-Style Anime · Technical Reference

---

## I. Color Palette and Visual Tone

- **Color Foundation** — The film utilizes a core color palette: Moon White (C1 #E8EAF5), Cyan-Green (C2 #4A9B8A), Vermillion (C3 #C93752), Indigo (C4 #2B4C7E), Golden Yellow (C5 #D4AF37), Ink Black (C6 #1A1A2E), Rouge (C7 #A94A5F), Ochre (C8 #965E3E), Gamboge (C9 #F5E375), and Grey Purple (C10 #7B6C85). The overall color temperature is neutral (5000-5600K) with mid-to-high saturation (55-70%), producing a rich, Eastern classical aesthetic typical of the New Traditional-Style.
- **Warm/Cool Narrative Contrast** — Warm colors (Vermillion C3, Golden Yellow C5, Rouge C7, Gamboge C9) serve as visual signals for positive emotions, luxury, or celebration. Cool colors (Indigo C4, Ink Black C6, Cyan-Green C2) serve as signals for conflict, lethality, or loneliness. Transitions between warm and cool should align with the story arc, not be mixed indiscriminately.
- **Warm/Cool Distribution Strategy** — Use cool tones (Indigo + Ink Black + Cyan-Green) for martial arts lethality or melancholic isolation scenes, prohibiting warm lighting like Vermillion/Golden Yellow. Use soft warm tones (Moon White + Rouge + Gamboge) for ethereal Xianxia or lighthearted daily life scenes. Use Vermillion + Golden Yellow highlights for luxurious court or festive celebration scenes.
- **Palette-First Principle** — Sequence planning must first link emotional scenarios (e.g., Ethereal Xianxia, Luxurious Court, Lethal Martial Arts, Daily Life, Poetic Moonlit Night, Festive Celebration) before determining the primary/secondary colors and lighting scheme. This prevents "matching the plot but missing the emotional color tone."
- **Restricted Color Gamut** — High-saturation fluorescent colors, neon colors, and colors with a strong digital feel are incompatible with this style. Hue shift tolerance is ±8°, saturation shift ±10%, and lightness shift ±12%.

---

## II. Lighting Scheme System

- **Lighting as Narrative** — 7 lighting schemes correspond to different emotional segments. During the planning phase, the director should determine the lighting tone at the sequence level rather than specifying it shot-by-shot.
- **Cel-Shading as Anchor** — All lighting schemes must maintain a flat cel-shaded texture. Shadows should use darker shades of the base color; solid black shadows are prohibited. Volumetric lighting and depth-of-field blurring are permitted as part of the Japanese-style rendering techniques.
- **Light Angle** — The default for front-lit shots is a 38.5° slanted soft light (cel-shaded texture); this can be adjusted for side-lit shots.

| Lighting Scheme | Name | Tone Tendency | Applicable Emotion |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Soft & Clear | Moon White base + soft Rouge skin light | Daily life, heartwarming sweetness, healing freshness |
| B | Ethereal | Moon White + Cyan-Green + Golden rim light | Xianxia elegance, ethereal atmosphere, stunning reveals |
| C | Candlelight | Ochre warmth dominant + Ink Black shadows | Nighttime intimacy, deepening bonds, gentle warmth |
| D | Moonlight | Indigo + Moon White cold light | Poetic moonlight, longing/solitude, quiet chill |
| E | Window Light | Moon White base + mottled side light | Interior quarters, study, daytime tranquility |
| F | Court Grandeur | Vermillion + Golden highlights + Ink Black shadows | Luxurious court, grand celebrations, formal solemnity |
| G | Cold Lethality | Indigo + Ink Black + 38.5° side-lit cold light, no warm colors | Martial arts duels, mournful isolation, tense atmosphere |

- **Warm/Cool Light Distribution** — Warm light (Ochre/Vermillion/Golden) is for positive emotions/luxurious scenes; martial arts/lethal/melancholic scenes must use cool light. Directors may adjust switching points based on narrative needs.
- **Atmospheric Mapping** — Every scene's atmosphere must map to one of the lighting schemes (A-G) to ensure visual consistency.

---

## III. Texture Direction

- **Cel-Shading as Anchor** — The core of the Traditional-Style Anime aesthetic: delicate lines, cel-shading, Japanese-style animation rendering, smooth gradients of color blocks, and no color banding or spillover.
- **Delicate Brushwork + Cinematic Quality** — This is the anchor for screen texture. It is not realistic photography, nor is it 3D CG; it is a fusion of Japanese animation rendering and traditional aesthetics.
- **"Anime-fying" Materials** — All costumes, makeup, and props must be rendered with cel-shading: silk luster via smooth color blocks, metal reflection via cool-toned sheen lines, and jade translucency via internal glow blocks. "3D-look" and "photorealistic" textures are prohibited.
- **Skin Texture** — Pink-white base, cel-shaded, natural luster, soft texture, internal glow, and skin clarity. Matte, death-white, waxy, greasy, or overexposed skin is prohibited.
- **Not Just "Flat"** — New Traditional-Style Anime emphasizes depth and cinematic quality via lighting layers, depth-of-field, and volumetric light, rather than a flat, depthless cartoon style.

---

## IV. Traditional-Style Anime Spatial Elements

Unique environmental elements and their visual narrative functions:

- **Sheer Curtains/Screens/Door Frames/Carved Lattice Windows** — Natural framing tools to create "semi-obscured" depth and spatial layering, with delicate carving details rendered via cel-shaded lines.
- **Courtyards/Flowering Trees/Rain Screens/Cyan-Green Landscapes** — Natural canvases for "negative space" composition where the scenery *is* the mood: Full blooming courtyard = Relief; Sitting alone in the rain = Loneliness; Falling leaves = Parting sorrow; Mist and clouds = Ethereal Xianxia.
- **Candlelight/Moonlight/Window Light/Lanterns** — Light carriers: Candlelight = Warm/Private (Scheme C), Moonlight = Cold/Solitary (Scheme D), Window Light = Daily/Quiet (Scheme E), Lanterns = Celebration/Marketplace (Scheme F).
- **Overhanging Eaves/Vermillion Walls/Stone Bridges/Classical Furniture** — Architectural elements presented in the New Traditional aesthetic: detail-rich, clear cel-shaded lines, with natural signs of wear and age. Brand-new, "plastic-looking" finishes are prohibited.
- **Transitions via Empty Shots** — This style features a wealth of environmental assets (variations of time/weather/shot sizes). Use empty environmental shots as emotional buffers between sequences instead of hard cuts.
- **Visuals Over Dialogue** — Prioritize visual storytelling (light shifts, jump cuts, metaphorical empty shots) rather than relying on dialogue for explanation.
- **Foreground, Midground, Background** — All scenes must possess three layers of depth. Flatness is forbidden; outdoor scenes must include atmospheric perspective (e.g., distant mountains fading into grey).

---

## V. Traditional Musical Instruments and Ambient Sound

Constraints on audio elements in the traditional-style world:

### Instrument Selection

- **Xiao (Vertical Flute)** — Core instrument for bleak, lonely, or mournful sequences; highly compatible with moonlight poetry or ethereal Xianxia atmospheres.
- **Erhu** — Used for stirring, sorrowful, or longing sequences; the "weeping" quality of the bow is ideal for emotional outbursts.
- **Suona** — Used for intense emotional moments (great joy/tragedy, fate shifts, climaxes); use sparingly, as it is a "nuke" of emotional intensity.
- **Guqin** — For setting the tone, steady sequences, or Xianxia ambiance; often paired with the Xiao.
- **Pipa** — Accents for tense or hurried sequences; acts as a rhythm driver for martial arts duels, not a lead instrument.
- String ensembles can enhance the cinematic feel but should not overshadow the primary melody.

### Instrument Combination Strategy

| Emotional Phase | Instrument Combination |
|---|---|
| Steady/Opening/Ending | Guqin solo or Guqin + Xiao |
| Xianxia/Ethereal/Atmospheric | Xiao + Guqin + Light Bells |
| Growing Sorrow | Xiao + Erhu |
| Emotional Outburst/Fate Shift | Suona solo or Suona + Erhu |
| Tense/Hurried/Martial Arts | Pipa accents + Percussion + String background |
| Daily Life/Sweetness | Guzheng + Bamboo Flute |
| Court/Celebration/Grandeur | Bronze Bells + Guzheng + String backing |

### Traditional Ambient Sound

- **Typical Ambient Layers** — Cicadas/insects, bubbling streams, wind through bamboo, marketplace vendors, night rain on eaves, friction of clothing, gentle wind chimes, falling flower petals, faint flickering of fireflies.
- **Label 1-2 core ambient sounds for every scene** to guide subsequent sound design. The richer the ambient layering, the more immersive the traditional-style scene will be.

## I. Purpose of the Storyboard Table

The storyboard is the core tool for a director to translate a script into cinematic language. The following guidelines provide constraint specifications for the "New Chinese Style" (Guofeng) anime aesthetic at the storyboard level.

## II. Lighting and Atmosphere

- **Cel-Shaded Lighting Consistency** — Lighting schemes must maintain cel-shading flatness throughout a single scene. Shadows must use a darkened shade of the base color (hard black shadows are prohibited) unless there is a clear narrative shift (e.g., candle extinguishing → cold moonlight).
- **Emotional Color Palette Binding** — Each scene must be bound to at least one emotional setting (e.g., Ethereal Xianxia/Palace Grandeur/Martial Arts Lethality/Girl's Daily Life/Moonlit Poetry/Festive Celebration). Maintain consistency of primary and secondary colors within a sequence, drawn from the core palette: Moon White #E8EAF5 / Cyan-Green #4A9B8A / Vermilion #C93752 / Indigo #2B4C7E / Golden #D4AF37 / Ink Black #1A1A2E / Rouge #A94A5F / Ochre #965E3E / Gamboge #F5E375 / Gray-Purple #7B6C85.
- **Lighting Angle Specifications** — The default for front-facing shots is 38.5° oblique soft lighting (for cel-shaded texture). Adjust for side-lit shots as required by the frame. When describing lighting, distinguish between front and side angles; rendering should always maintain a Japanese-animation texture.
- **Matching Color Temperature to Narrative Stages** — Use cool tones (Indigo + Ink Black + Cyan-Green) for martial arts lethality or melancholic segments; forbid warm orange/vermilion highlights. Use soft warm tones (Moon White + Rouge + Gamboge) for ethereal Xianxia or daily life segments. Use Vermilion + Golden highlights for palace grandeur.
- **Lighting Transitions as Advanced Narrative Tools** — A gradient from sheer curtain daylight to warm candlelight denotes the passage of time (Day to Night). Mark lighting transition points in the storyboard; cel-shaded color blocks must transition smoothly, with no color banding or spillover.
- **Anime-Style Rendering Effects** — Reasonable use of volumetric light, depth-of-field blur, and rim lighting is permitted, but must maintain the aesthetic foundation of "Guofeng" cel-shading. 3D-realistic or CGI animation light effects are prohibited.

## III. Environmental Dynamics

- **Environmental Dynamics for "Breathable" Frames** — Falling petals, swirling mist, rippling water, fluttering sheer curtains, and drifting robes. Include an environment-dynamic shot at least every 3-4 frames to prevent the scene from feeling "dead."
- **Prioritize Guofeng Anime Elements** — Use environmental dynamics consistent with the Guofeng worldview: falling flowers, floating catkins, light smoke, flowing water, wind through bamboo, flickering candlelight, moving clouds, fireflies, falling snow. Modern elements are prohibited.
- **Cel-Shaded Dynamic Texture** — Dynamic elements (petals/smoke/water, etc.) must retain cel-shaded, flat-painted textures and delicate lines. 3D particle effects or photorealistic fluid simulations are prohibited.

---

## IV. Guofeng Anime Action Rhythm

- **Elegant and Stylized Action** — Character actions should default to being slow and elegant, but allow for exaggerated "anime-style" keyframes (e.g., sharp, crisp lines in martial arts duels, hair fluttering during a character's gaze). Movements like rising, turning, or lifting a hand should be annotated as "slow/elegant."
- **Garment Dynamics as Core Assets** — The movement of Guofeng attire (wide sleeves, skirts, ribbons, sashes) is a natural dynamic asset. Incorporate these into frame descriptions. Under cel-shading, garment movement should have clear, distinct lines and layering.
- **Demeanor Constraints** — The conduct of characters should reflect classical poise: walking should be steady and slow; gestures should be subtle and restrained. Avoid modern body language (e.g., shrugging, spreading hands). Anime characters may show slight exaggeration in micro-expressions (crescent-moon eyes, blushing), but body movements must remain classically restrained.
- **Consistent Proportions** — Characters must maintain 6-7 head-height proportions throughout action designs. Do not break these proportions for the sake of dynamic poses.

---

## V. Material and Texture Constraints

- **Cel-Shaded Flatness as the Anchor** — Rendering for all characters, costumes, props, and environments must maintain a cel-shaded texture with delicate, clear lines and smooth color blocks. 3D-realistic/CGI/photorealistic rendering is prohibited.
- **Garment Materials** — Express the textures of silk, embroidery, and pearlescent fabrics using cel-shading; textures must be ultra-clear.
- **Prop Materials** — Metallic cold sheen, translucent jade, clear wood grain, and warm porcelain should be rendered with cel-shading and delicate brushwork. Realistic material rendering is prohibited.
- **Environment Materials** — Surfaces should show signs of wear, weathering, or natural folds, presenting a sense of age while maintaining the Guofeng anime brush style. Avoid "plastic-like" or "flawless new" materials.

---

## VI. Color Constraints

- **Core Palette Locking** — Colors for all shots in the storyboard must be selected from the core palette (C1-C10). Hue offset tolerance: ±8°, Saturation offset: ±10%, Brightness offset: ±12%.

- **Line Color** — Use Ink Black or Dark Brown; pure black thick lines are prohibited.
- **Shadow Color** — Use a darkened shade of the same hue; hard black shadows are prohibited.
